Timeline

1989-1900

Charlie Chaplin was born on April 16, 1889, in London, England. His early years were marked by poverty and hardship. Raised in a family of entertainers, he developed an interest in performance from a young age

1901-1913

Chaplin joined a clog-dancing troupe at the age of eight, starting his journey into entertainment. By 17, he had become a seasoned performer in British music halls, gaining valuable experience in comedy and pantomime.

1914-1917

In 1913, Chaplin signed with Keystone Studios in Hollywood, marking the beginning of his film career. His iconic character, the Tramp, made its debut in "Kid Auto Races at Venice" (1914). This period saw rapid success, with Chaplin becoming a global sensation

1918-1922

Chaplin formed his own production company, United Artists, in 1919, along with Mary Pickford, Douglas Fairbanks, and D.W. Griffith. This move granted him unprecedented creative control over his films. During this time, he created masterpieces like "The Kid" (1921) and "The Gold Rush" (1925)

1923-1936

The transition to sound posed a challenge for many silent film stars, but Chaplin embraced it with "City Lights" (1931) and "Modern Times" (1936). Despite the advent of talkies, Chaplin's Tramp remained a silent character, showcasing his unique ability to communicate through physical comedy.

1937-1952

Chaplin faced political controversies during the McCarthy era due to his leftist views and refusal to become a U.S. citizen. This led to his self-imposed exile from the United States in 1952. During this period, he created the satirical masterpiece "The Great Dictator" (1940).

1953-1977

Chaplin returned to the U.S. in 1972 to receive an honorary Academy Award. He continued working on films like "A King in New York" (1957) and "A Countess from Hong Kong" (1967). Charlie Chaplin passed away on December 25, 1977, leaving behind a cinematic legacy that continues to influence and inspire generations of filmmakers and performers worldwide.

Charlie Chaplin

(16 April 1889 - 25 December 1977)

Sir Charles Spencer Chaplin aka Charlie Chaplin was an English comic actor, filmmaker, and composer who rose to fame in the era of silent film. He became a worldwide icon through his screen persona, the Tramp, and is considered one of the film industry's most important figures. His career spanned more than 75 years, from childhood in the Victorian era until a year before his death in 1977, and encompassed both adulation and controversy
